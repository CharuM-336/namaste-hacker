### PART 1 — What is Experience DNA?
- **Definition:** Structured, AI‑driven description of *how* a book should be lived, not just *what* it contains.
- **Why Book DNA isn’t enough:** It captures text and structure but lacks emotional tone, pacing, interaction, and visual intent.
- **Why every book needs a unique experience:** Genres and authorial intent demand distinct emotional, kinetic, and spatial cues.
- **Difference from themes/templates:** Themes are superficial style skins; Experience DNA is a **semantic contract** that drives motion, navigation, companion, and visual language.
- **Bridge role:** Translates AI‑derived understanding of the book into concrete UI directives for a renderer.

### PART 2 — Experience Reasoning Engine (high‑level flow)
1. Ingest Book DNA.
2. Classify genre & authorial intent.
3. Generate an **Experience Dimensions** vector (Wonder, Curiosity, …).
4. Match one or more **Experience Archetypes**.
5. Choose a navigation system.
6. Synthesize Companion DNA.
7. Select motion motifs.
8. Build a scene blueprint.
9. Compute Experience Scores (Wonder, Reflection, …).
10. Emit the **Experience DNA JSON** contract.

### PART 10 — Visual Language Principles
1. Typography mirrors narrative voice.
2. Spacing encodes pacing (tight = fast, wide = slow).
3. Rhythm follows Experience Scores (Energy → fast transitions).
4. Margins act as cognitive buffers.
5. Card modularity scales with **Density**.
6. Depth conveys hierarchy (elevation = importance).
7. Lighting signals focus (spotlight → focus, ambient → background).
8. Texture suggests materiality (paper, glass, metal).
9. White space is a narrative pause.
10. Visual hierarchy aligns with **Emotional Goal** (Wonder → large focal elements).
